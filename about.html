We started with a bowler hat on a t-shirt. We’re still at it.

EST. 2008 — LONDON

How it started.

It started with the bowler-hatted skull. London, 2008 — a small run, no plan, word of mouth. The design landed because it said something without explaining itself. That’s still the test.

The brand went quiet for a while. Came back harder. New designs, same idea: take something genuinely British — a battle queen, a mod target, a gent who won’t be rattled — and put it on a tee that wears better the longer you have it.

The characters were already there. We just put them on cotton.

THE PRODUCT

What we make.

220gsm heavyweight ringspun cotton. Printed in Britain. Ribbed crew neck, taped shoulders, the kind of weight that holds its shape after a hundred washes.

We don’t do seasonal. We don’t do trends. When a design makes the cut, it stays until it doesn’t make sense any more — which hasn’t happened yet.

THE NAME

What it means.

Blitz spirit: what you need when your back is against the wall. Not a stiff upper lip — that’s the mask. Blitz spirit is what’s underneath. The decision to carry on, find the funny side, put the kettle on and sort it out.

It’s not nostalgia. Boudica proved it in AD 60. The mods proved it in 1964. The City gent walking to work through rubble in 1940 proved it. Same character, different century.

That’s what the brand is. One idea, four designs (and counting), on the best cotton we could find.
